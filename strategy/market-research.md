# MailFilter — Market Research (March 2026)

## Direct Competitors

| Player | Status | Model | Key Insight |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|
| **PaperKarma** | Active, 10+ years | $3.99/mo, 100K+ mailers in DB | Market leader but mediocre reviews. Users resent paying; daily photo requirement causes churn |
| **PostGhost** | Open beta Jan 2025 | AI snap-and-submit + data broker removal | Closest competitor to MailFilter. Claims 80% reduction in 8 weeks. 12-18 months ahead |
| **DMAchoice** | Active, industry-run | $6 one-time, 10-year opt-out | Only covers ANA members. Industry policing itself |
| **CatalogChoice** | Active, nonprofit | Free, 2M+ users | Manual, narrow scope (catalogs only) |
| **OptOutPrescreen** | Active | Free | Only prescreened credit/insurance offers |
| **DoNotPay** | Active but FTC-sanctioned | Subscription | Fined $193K by FTC for deceptive AI claims. Cautionary tale |

## Market Data

- **81% of Americans** support a Do Not Mail registry
- **50M+** USPS Informed Delivery users
- **$37B/year** spent on direct mail advertising (growing — 82% of enterprise marketers increased budgets in 2024)
- **41 lbs** of junk mail per household per year
- **80B+ pieces** of junk mail annually in the US
- **100M trees** cut down per year for junk mail
- **12+ states** have introduced Do Not Mail legislation (none passed yet)

## Structural Challenges

1. **EDDM (Every Door Direct Mail) cannot be stopped.** Addressed to "Postal Customer," not an individual. No legal opt-out mechanism.
2. **USPS earns $15.7B/year from marketing mail** (~20% of revenue). Zero incentive to help.
3. **No federal law compels mailers to honor opt-outs** (except prescreened credit offers under FCRA).
4. **Direct mail industry is growing**, not shrinking. Mailers investing more.
5. **PaperKarma backlash** — consumers resent paying to stop mail they never asked for.

## Viability Assessment

**Viable but structurally limited.** Real pain, real demand, but can't promise to eliminate all junk mail.

**Strongest argument FOR:** Existing solutions are fragmented and mediocre. No single product does AI classification + multi-channel opt-out + Informed Delivery integration + transparent progress tracking.

**Strongest argument AGAINST:** The fundamental opt-out mechanism is voluntary. EDDM is unstoppable. The $37B industry has every incentive to keep mailing. Customer satisfaction has a structural ceiling.

**Winning strategy:** AI classification + multi-channel opt-outs + upstream data broker removal + radical transparency about what can/can't be stopped.
